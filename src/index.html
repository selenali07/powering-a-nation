Hawaii’s Beaches Are Disappearing...

Hawaii is famous for its spectacular beaches. But over the past century, three of the state’s major islands, Oahu, Maui and Kauai, have lost roughly one-quarter of their sandy shores.

Read More

Despite the "no tolerance" policy set in 1999. Officials have continually allowed people to build new seawalls and reconstruct older, crumbling ones while approving illegally built structures after the fact.

Seawalls erode Hawaii’s beaches, but the state has been lax about approving them and disorganized about enforcing the law. Officials now pledge action, after a Honolulu Star-Advertiser and ProPublica investigation.

The loss of Hawaii’s beaches threatens the way of life in the islands and imperils the state’s tourist-driven economy.
